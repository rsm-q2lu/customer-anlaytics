perf_calc = function (df,target_customers,margin_on_sales,cost_to_push,sms,intro) {
mailto_prop=sum(df$mailto)/nrow(df)
mailed_customers=mailto_prop*target_customers
mailed=df %>% filter(mailto==TRUE)
expected_response_rate=mean(mailed$buyer=='yes')
exp_respon_cus=expected_response_rate*mailed_customers
mailing_cost=mailed_customers*cost_to_push
profit=exp_respon_cus*margin_on_sales-mailing_cost
ROME=profit/mailing_cost
result=data.frame(sms=sms,intro=intro,expected_response_rate=expected_response_rate,exp_respon_cus=exp_respon_cus,mailing_cost=mailing_cost,profit=profit,ROME=ROME)
return (result)
}

res_iq=perf_calc(mailto_iq,target_customers,margin_on_sales,cost_to_push,sms = "smsto_iq", intro = "Based on independent quantile RFM")
res_sq=perf_calc(mailto_sq,target_customers,margin_on_sales,cost_to_push,sms = "smsto_sq", intro = "Based on sequantial quantile RFM")

diff_iq_sq=rbind(res_sq,res_iq)

What do you see as the pros and cons of the two approaches (from a statistical as well as logical perspective) and why?

Independent index:
Pros: Logically,easier to interpret. It simply divided the all of people into groups in terms of F,R,M.
Cons: Statistically, it makes the proportion of buyers less evenly distributed in different RFM index

Sequential index:
Pros: Statistically, it makes the proportion of buyers more evenly distributed in different RFM index. In this case, the profit and ROME are higher than those of independent approach.
Cons: Logically, it’s more complicated to explain than independent index. If people buy something cheap frequently. He is compared with people who buy cheap stuffs rather than with all of the people.

If we use standard error to set a lowerbound of the response rate to compare the breakeven response rate, we will send fewer sms. That will reduce our mailing cost. The expected response rate will increase.
But since we send to fewer customers, the total responded customers will be decreased and obviously the shrinking revenue caused shrinking profit.
Although the ROMEs are higher than question9, the profits are lower. So I think we’d better not to use the lowerbound method.
